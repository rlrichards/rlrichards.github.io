About Me

I'm a PhD student at the University of Georgia's Odum School of Ecology working with John Drake and Vanessa Ezenwa. My interests center on the community ecology of disease transmission. I combine field data with quantitative methods ranging from mathematical transmission models to machine learning algorithms to ask these questions. My PhD work focuses on the role of predators in driving prevalences and intensities of macroparasites of rodents using a long-term predator exclusion experiment at the Joseph W. Jones Ecological Research Center. Side projects largely involve developing and implementing comuptational methods to understand spatial heterogeneities in disease transmission, ranging from macroparasites of ungulates on the National Bison Range to Guinnea worm (Dracunculus medinensis) in domestic dog populations in Chad.
